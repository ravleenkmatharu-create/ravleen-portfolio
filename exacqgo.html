Johnson Controls — ExacqGo

Mobile-first, gesture-driven app for managing secure access across employees, admins, and visitors. Built in collaboration with Johnson Controls to transform smartphones into secure digital credentials.

Role UX Researcher & Designer
Timeline 9 Months — Fall 2024 to Spring 2025
Team 3 design students + JC teams
Platform iOS & Android
Tools Figma · FigJam · Cursor AI · GitHub · Expo

Overview

Johnson Controls is a global leader in smart building technologies, operating in over 150 countries with solutions spanning physical security, energy efficiency, and sustainable infrastructure. Their mission: merge innovation with trust by designing secure, user-centered systems where safety, efficiency, and usability coexist. The challenge: enhance mobile access within their ExacqVision platform to enable secure smartphone-based door access, replacing traditional keycards and fobs with a seamless, gesture-driven experience.

The Problem

Despite robust security infrastructure, Johnson Controls' existing access management faced critical usability challenges that directly impacted user trust and adoption. Research revealed three core pain points that consistently hindered both daily operations and new user onboarding.

Design Challenge

"How might we create a mobile access control system that is secure and intuitive for managing physical access across work environments?"

Research & Discovery

Understanding User Needs & Market

To better understand the real-world challenges users face with access management systems, interviews were conducted with key stakeholders, system integrators, and providers. The goal was to identify pain points, unmet needs, and expectations across different roles—from IT employees to security managers to building managers.

01

Secondary Research. Analysis of current trends in mobile-first access control, user-centered security design, and enterprise authentication. Key findings revealed growing demand for IoT-based solutions driven by energy efficiency, compliance, and data security in user-friendly, adaptable systems.

02

Primary Research. In-depth interviews examining how users interact with current systems, identifying pain points around data access and usability, and uncovering expectations for future improvements. Economic stabilization and growth have made investors optimistic about enterprise security solutions.

03

Field Observations. Studying real-world usage patterns in commercial and residential settings, documenting friction points in daily workflows, and identifying moments of delight or frustration.

Competitive Analysis

Evaluated 10+ competitors (Suprema, Kisi, Gatewise, Lenel S2, Brivo, Bosch, HID, ButterflyMX, SoloInsight) across affordability, mobile integration, UI quality, and integration capabilities.

01

Key Competitor Limitations. Lack of customization and scalability for diverse enterprise needs. Heavy hardware dependency reducing flexibility. Fragmented visitor management leading to inconsistent experience and security risks.

02

Emerging Market Demands. Cloud-first architectures enabling remote management and real-time updates. Biometric and touchless access methods driven by post-pandemic hygiene concerns. Streamlined onboarding and visitor workflows to reduce friction.

03

Market Gap Identified. The intersection of high mobile credential strength + high integration capability represents a relatively uncrowded opportunity with significant growth potential.

04

ExacqGo's Position. Built to address this gap by combining enterprise-grade security, intuitive mobile UX, and deep integration with JC's existing ecosystem.

Key Insight

No existing competitor combines high mobile credential strength with deep integration capability, this is ExacqGo's strategic opportunity.

Solution

ExacqGo: Key Features

ExacqGo transforms any smartphone into a secure digital credential. The core idea: eliminate dependency on physical ID cards and create a fluid, user-centered mobile experience where users store digital credentials in Apple Wallet or Google Wallet, powered by enterprise-grade encryption and cloud-based authorization.

01

Home Dashboard

A centralized screen displaying real-time status of favorite doors—Locked, Unlocked, Restricted, or In Range. Users unlock doors, request access, or take quick actions directly from the dashboard. Add guests to upcoming visitor lists for seamless visitor management.

02 Signature Feature

Gesture Unlock

Users unlock doors by pointing their phone at the reader and twisting their wrist, mimicking the motion of turning a key. This natural gesture provides quick, touchless access without needing to open the app or press a button. Intuitive, secure, and delightful.

03

Visitor Flow

Hosts invite guests directly from the app by entering their name, visit time, and access area. Visitors receive an email with details and a temporary digital access card they can add to Apple Wallet or Google Wallet. Upon arrival, they simply tap their phone—no manual check-in needed.

04

Door Access & Real-Time Alerts

Users view real-time status of nearby doors and perform quick actions like unlocking, requesting access, or reporting an issue. Live camera feeds available when configured. Contextual alerts for routine events and detailed drill-downs for incidents requiring action.

Validation

Outcomes & Testing

Testing Methods

01

Post-Test Surveys. Participants completed System Usability Scale (SUS) and Single Ease Question (SEQ) to quantify usability and perceived task effort.

02

Cognitive Walkthrough. Internal team ran scenario-based walkthroughs assessing learnability using the PURE (Practical Usability Rating by Experts) model.

03

Expert Review. Designers and IT employees conducted heuristic evaluations validating interface logic and accessibility standards.

04

Think-Aloud Protocol. Users narrated their thought process while performing key tasks, revealing friction points around labeling, button discoverability, and navigation.

Comprehensive usability testing across multiple methods and user types validated both the functional design and user delight factor. Results demonstrated excellent usability scores well above industry benchmarks.
