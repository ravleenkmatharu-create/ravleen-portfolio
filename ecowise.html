Overview

The Problem

Energy Insight needed a platform that could turn raw energy consumption data into meaningful, actionable insights. Existing tools were either too technical for everyday users or too simplified to drive real behavioral change. The addition of generative AI created new UX challenges around trust, transparency, and conversational interfaces.

The Goal

Design a web platform that makes energy monitoring intuitive and engaging, integrating an AI assistant that users can trust and converse with naturally — ultimately driving smarter energy decisions for homes and businesses.

Discovery

Design Process

Designing for AI Transparency

The core UX challenge was integrating AI without creating a "black box" experience. I explored multiple patterns for showing AI reasoning, tested different conversational UI models, and refined based on usability testing.

01

Competitive Analysis

Audited 6 existing energy platforms and 4 AI assistant products to identify patterns and gaps in the market.

02

Persona Development

Built two core personas — the data-driven optimizer and the goal-oriented saver — to guide feature prioritization.

03

Dashboard Architecture

Designed an information hierarchy that surfaces the most actionable data first, with drill-down for advanced users.

04

AI Assistant UX

Iterated on conversational UI patterns, testing how to communicate AI confidence levels and explain recommendations clearly.

Solution

Key Design Decisions

📊

Real-Time Monitoring Dashboard

A live dashboard surfacing energy consumption by appliance, room, and time period. Color-coded indicators make status immediately scannable without reading numbers.

🤖

Generative AI Assistant

A conversational AI that answers natural language questions about energy usage, provides explainable recommendations, and adapts suggestions to household goals — with transparent reasoning shown for every suggestion.

🎯

Goal-Setting & Progress Tracking

Users set savings goals (reduce bill by 20%, cut carbon footprint) and the platform tracks progress with contextual nudges — meeting both persona types where they are.

Reflection

What I Learned

Designing with AI pushed me to think beyond screens. The hardest problem wasn't visual design — it was building user trust in an opaque system. Every design decision had to answer: "Does this make the AI feel more trustworthy or less?" That framing changed how I approached every interaction in the product. I also learned the value of designing for two distinct mental models simultaneously — personalization is not just a nice-to-have, it's sometimes the only path to genuine usefulness.
