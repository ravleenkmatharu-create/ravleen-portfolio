Energy Insight — EcoWise

An intelligent, AI-enhanced platform enabling manufacturing teams to monitor, troubleshoot, and optimize energy usage independently — with real-time insights and a built-in generative AI assistant.

Role UI/UX Researcher & Designer
Timeline 3 Months — Spring 2024
Team 3 design students + Energy Insight team
Platform Web Dashboard
Tools Figma · Miro · Notion
Overview

Project Overview

Energy Insights is an Indiana-based energy technology company providing IoT-powered solutions for small to mid-sized manufacturers. While their original starter kit helped reduce energy costs, the platform faced critical challenges in usability, long-term support, and business scalability after grant funding ended. The team reimagined the solution as EcoWise — a scalable B2B SaaS platform featuring real-time insights, intelligent alerts, and a built-in generative AI assistant that enables manufacturers to operate independently with minimal external support or training.

3

design students collaborating with the Energy Insight team to reimagine the platform over 3 months.

B2B

SaaS platform built for industrial manufacturers operating in smart manufacturing environments.

4

core platform features: streamlined onboarding, machine-level dashboard, AI Nova assistant, and smart alerts.

Challenge

The Challenge

The original platform helped reduce energy costs, but faced critical barriers: usability issues prevented operators from interpreting energy data, long-term support was inconsistent, and business scalability faltered after grant funding ended. Manufacturing teams needed a solution that delivered independent value without relying on consultants or extensive training.

Root Challenge

Usability issues prevented operators from interpreting energy data, long-term support was inconsistent, and business scalability faltered after grant funding ended.

Design Challenge

"How might we support manufacturing teams in understanding energy usage and maintaining efficiency after the grant period, without relying on constant external support or complex dashboards?"

Research

Research & Discovery

To understand real-world challenges, interviews were conducted with key stakeholders, system integrators, and hardware/software providers. Secondary research analyzed industry trends and economic forces. Primary research involved in-depth interviews examining how users interact with current energy systems.

01

Secondary Research. Industry trends, market demands, competitive landscape, and economic forces shaping the smart energy sector reveal growing IoT demand driven by efficiency, compliance, and data security requirements.

02

Stakeholder Interviews. Conversations with system integrators, hardware/software providers, and energy consultants identified unmet needs, pain points around data access and usability, and expectations across different manufacturing roles.

03

Observation & Analysis. In-depth examination of how users interact with current energy systems, identifying barriers in data interpretation and uncovering expectations for AI-powered insights and automation.

Ideation

Ideation & Concept Development

With core problem areas identified, the team entered the ideation phase, blending problem framing and brainstorming in a collaborative, hands-on sprint. The Crazy 8's method was used to quickly explore a wide range of solutions, from AI-powered chat assistants to adaptive dashboards that respond to user roles.

01

Crazy 8s

Rapid ideation sprint exploring 8 variations per concept across onboarding, dashboards, alerts, and AI features.

02

HMW Prompts

Reframing barriers into opportunity-focused design questions that guide solution direction.

03

Stakeholder Reviews

Aligning insights through peer critiques and client feedback to validate design direction.

04

Role Mapping

Defining distinct user flows for operators, engineers, and management to address varying needs.

What Emerged

Through stakeholder interviews and peer critiques, four key patterns emerged:

Scale Beyond Pilots Systems must scale beyond pilot phases and reduce reliance on consultants
Role-Specific Interfaces Different users require role-specific interfaces based on their responsibilities
Automated Support Support should be automated, contextual, and continuous
Long-Term Value The solution must provide long-term value, not just static data
Insights

Key Insights

These patterns became the guiding principles for the entire platform architecture. Every feature—from the dashboard to the AI Nova assistant—was designed to address these core needs.

Core Principle

The solution must deliver long-term value, not just display static data. Support and insights should be automated, contextual, and continuous — enabling independent operation without reliance on external consultants or extensive training.

Solution

Solution

EcoWise delivers a suite of integrated features that empower manufacturers to achieve lasting improvements in energy management and efficiency. Each feature is designed to reduce manual burden and enable confident, independent decision-making.

01

Streamlined Onboarding & First-Time Setup

New users are welcomed with a guided onboarding flow designed to minimize setup time and build confidence from the start. Walks users through selecting their role and energy priorities, auto-configures dashboard views and alert settings based on preferences, and provides a step-by-step tour of key features, including AI Nova and machine-level insights.

02

Dashboard with Machine-Level Focus

The dashboard provides a live snapshot of energy usage across the manufacturing floor, enabling users to monitor, compare, and act on machine performance. Displays energy status and alerts for each machine in a simplified, visual layout. Let users click into individual machines for detailed metrics and historical trends. Offers filter and sorting options to prioritize what needs attention most.

03 Signature Feature

AI Nova Assistant

AI Nova is a built-in, conversational assistant that provides real-time support, helping users make sense of their energy data and resolve issues more quickly. True to its name, Nova's mission is to bring clarity at critical moments, illuminating complex insights and guiding users toward confident, informed action. Offers step-by-step troubleshooting, learns from plant-level behavior, and acts as a knowledge hub reducing reliance on external support.

04

Smart Alert System: Real-Time Voltage Warnings

Our IoT-powered system continuously monitors electrical health to detect anomalies and prevent issues before they escalate. Detects abnormal behavior like consistently elevated voltage levels. Displays critical alerts with clear context—machine name, issue severity, and easy-to-understand explanations. Directs users to the integrated Help Center or AI Nova Assistant for instant support and guidance.

Business Strategy

Business Model

EcoWise is positioned as a scalable B2B SaaS platform designed for industrial clients operating in smart manufacturing environments. Built to support long-term client partnerships, the model is focused on recurring revenue, energy optimization, and operational efficiency.

01

Subscription-Based Tiers. Flexible pricing based on factory size, usage level, and feature needs. Entry-level plans include essential monitoring tools, while advanced tiers include AI diagnostics and AI Nova chatbot support.

02

AI-as-a-Service. Premium tools powered by AI Nova unlock predictive maintenance, root-cause analysis, and proactive alert systems to reduce downtime.

03

Integration Services. Premium onboarding and technical deployment ensure success with custom setup, implementation support, and seamless integration with IoT systems and Manufacturing Execution Systems platforms.

04

Data Analytics Add-Ons. Advanced analytics packages for large-scale manufacturers include benchmarking against industry standards, automated compliance reporting, and optimization consulting.
