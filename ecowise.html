Energy Insight — EcoWise

An intelligent, AI-enhanced platform that helps manufacturers monitor energy usage, troubleshoot issues, and optimize efficiency with minimal external support—powered by real-time insights and a built-in generative AI assistant.

Role UX Researcher & Designer
Timeline 3 Months — Spring 2024
Team 3 design students + Energy Insight
Platform Web Dashboard
Tools Figma · Miro · Notion

Context

Energy Insights' original solution helped manufacturers reduce energy costs through IoT-powered monitoring. However, when grant funding ended, the business model broke down. Users faced unexpected ongoing costs, poor onboarding support, and dashboards that required technical expertise to interpret.

The company needed to pivot from a hardware-first model to a sustainable SaaS platform—one that empowered users to achieve energy optimization independently, without relying on external consultants or extensive training.

This required reimagining the entire user experience: from the information architecture to the role-specific interfaces, and the integration of AI as a contextual support system rather than a novelty feature.

Research Findings

We conducted interviews with facility operators, plant engineers, and facility managers to understand energy management workflows, pain points, and mental models. Three core challenges emerged:

01

Support Gaps. Users were surprised by ongoing costs after grants ended and lacked post-launch service support.

02

Complexity Barriers. Operators couldn't interpret dense dashboards without extensive training or external guidance.

03

Divergent Needs. Operators, engineers, and managers had completely different mental models and required different interfaces.

04

Reliance on Consultants. The system couldn't scale because every issue required external expert intervention.

Through affinity mapping and synthesis, we formulated our problem statement:

"How might we support manufacturing teams in understanding energy usage and maintaining efficiency after grant funding, without relying on constant external support or complex dashboards?"

Synthesizing Insights

Our research revealed that one-size-fits-all design would fail. We mapped customer segments and their unique value propositions to understand how to serve three distinct user types within a unified platform.

Equally important: understanding how EcoWise fits within the larger IoT ecosystem. Data flows bidirectionally between machines, dashboards, and AI—each role accessing filtered views of the same intelligent backend.

This ecosystem thinking became the north star for design. The dashboard wasn't just a visualization layer—it was the interface to an intelligent system where data, alerts, and AI guidance work together to reduce user burden and enable independent decision-making.

Design Approach

Our solution centered on three strategic pillars: role-adaptive interfaces, machine-level clarity, and AI as a trusted partner rather than a black box.

After iterating on wireframes and testing with real manufacturers, we evolved the design to emphasize role-specific dashboards—where operators see simplified, task-focused views; engineers access detailed metrics and customization controls; and management views ROI dashboards. All powered by a unified intelligent backend.

The key innovation: instead of forcing all users into the same interface, EcoWise adapts. The same data flows through different lenses. Operators get clarity. Engineers get control. Management gets ROI. This required designing not just screens, but a system that responds to user mental models.

Solution

EcoWise delivers intelligent energy management through four core features that work together to reduce manual burden and enable confident, independent decision-making.

01

Guided Onboarding Flow

New users are welcomed with a step-by-step setup that walks them through role selection, energy priorities, and dashboard customization. The system auto-configures itself based on user type, eliminating the need for complex configuration or external training.

02

Machine-Centric Dashboard

Live energy status across the manufacturing floor. Click into any machine for detailed metrics, historical trends, and anomalies. The visual layout makes it easy to spot what needs attention at a glance, and role-specific views ensure users only see what matters to their job.

03

AI Nova Assistant

A conversational AI that provides real-time support without overwhelming users. Nova interprets energy data, guides troubleshooting, and learns from plant-level behavior to deliver smarter, personalized guidance over time.

04

Smart Alert System

Real-time voltage warnings and anomaly detection. Critical alerts include context, severity, and next steps. Contextual push notifications inform without overwhelming—brief alerts for routine events, detailed drill-downs for incidents requiring action.

Reflection

Designing with AI fundamentally changed how I approach product thinking. The hardest problem wasn't visual design—it was building user trust in an opaque system. Every design decision had to answer: "Does this make the AI feel trustworthy or suspicious?"

I also learned that personalization isn't optional when users have divergent mental models. Operators, engineers, and management literally think differently about energy data. Rather than compromise with a "one-size-fits-some" approach, we embraced role adaptation as a core strategy. This required designing for three distinct user experiences while maintaining a unified intelligent core.

💡
The best energy tool isn't a dashboard that shows more data—it's a partner that understands your role and shows you exactly what you need to know.
