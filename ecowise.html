Energy Insight — EcoWise

An intelligent, AI-enhanced platform for manufacturers — real-time monitoring, actionable insights, and a built-in generative AI assistant that makes energy optimization accessible to everyone.

Timeline Spring 2024 · 3 Months
Role UX Researcher & Designer
Team 3 design students + Energy Insight team
Platform Web Dashboard
Overview

The Challenge

Energy Insights' original solution helped manufacturers reduce energy costs, but faced critical barriers: steep learning curves, lack of ongoing support after grant funding ended, and information overload in dashboards. Users needed intuitive, self-service tools that didn't require external consultants.

The Solution

EcoWise reimagines energy monitoring as an AI-enhanced platform that combines real-time dashboards with an intelligent assistant. Manufacturers now get actionable insights, machine-level clarity, and contextual support—all without external dependencies.

Context

Designing for Urgency & Clarity

When grant funding ended, Energy Insights needed to transition from a hardware-first model to a sustainable SaaS platform. This meant reimagining the entire user experience — moving away from feature-dense dashboards toward role-specific interfaces powered by AI.

Research

Understanding Real Manufacturers

We conducted in-depth interviews with facility operators, plant engineers, and facility managers to understand their energy management workflows, pain points, and expectations.

💰

Support Gaps & Business Friction

Ongoing costs surprised users after grants ended. Poor installation support and costly maintenance without predictive tools created friction. Users needed transparent pricing and proactive guidance.

📚

Complexity & Training Barriers

Operators lacked technical skills to interpret dense dashboards. Information overload made decision-making difficult. The system needed built-in support, not external training.

👥

Divergent User Mental Models

Operators needed simple, role-specific dashboards. Engineers wanted customization and control. Management required ROI dashboards. One-size-fits-all design would fail.

Design Process

From Insights to Solution

We synthesized research into a core insight: the best energy tool isn't a dashboard—it's a partner. We built EcoWise around the belief that intelligent systems should reduce cognitive load, not increase it.

01

Problem Framing

Synthesized interview data into three core challenges: support gaps, training barriers, and divergent user needs. Identified AI as the key lever to provide contextual, role-aware assistance.

02

HMW Workshop

"How might we support manufacturing teams in understanding energy usage and maintaining efficiency after grant funding without relying on constant external support?"

03

Role-Based Design

Designed three distinct interfaces: Operator (simple, task-focused), Engineer (detailed metrics), Manager (ROI-focused). All powered by the same intelligent backend.

04

Iterative Validation

Tested prototypes with real manufacturers. Iterated on AI Nova responses, alert thresholds, and dashboard layouts based on feedback.

Solution

Key Features

01

Guided Onboarding Flow

New users are welcomed with a step-by-step setup that walks them through role selection, energy priorities, and dashboard customization. No training required—the system configures itself based on user needs.

02

Machine-Centric Dashboard

Live energy status across the manufacturing floor. Click into any machine for detailed metrics, historical trends, and anomalies. Visual layout makes it easy to spot what needs attention at a glance.

03

AI Nova Assistant

A conversational AI that provides real-time support without overwhelming users. Nova interprets energy data, guides troubleshooting, answers questions, and learns from plant-level behavior to deliver smarter guidance over time.

04

Smart Alert System

Real-time voltage warnings and anomaly detection. Critical alerts include context, severity, and next steps. Users know exactly what happened and what to do—no guessing required.

Reflection

What I Learned

Designing with AI fundamentally changed how I think about products. The hardest problem wasn't visual design—it was building user trust in an opaque system. Every design decision had to answer: "Does this make the AI feel trustworthy or suspicious?" That framing changed everything. I also learned that personalization isn't a nice-to-have when users have wildly different mental models—it's the only path to genuine usefulness.

💡
"The best energy tool isn't a dashboard that shows more data—it's a partner that understands your role and shows you exactly what you need to know."
