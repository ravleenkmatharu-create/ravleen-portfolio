Project Overview

The Company

Energy Insights is an Indiana-based energy technology company that provides IoT-powered solutions for small to mid-sized manufacturers. While their original starter kit helped reduce energy costs, the platform faced key challenges in usability, long-term support, and business scalability, especially after grant funding ended.

What We Built

Our team reimagined the solution as EcoWise, an intelligent, AI-enhanced platform featuring real-time insights and a built-in generative AI assistant. This redesigned experience enables manufacturers to monitor, troubleshoot, and optimize their energy usage independently, with minimal training or outside support.

Secondary Research

To ensure our redesign addresses real-world challenges and opportunities, we conducted comprehensive secondary research. This involved analyzing industry trends, market demands, economic forces, and the competitive landscape shaping the smart energy sector.

Increased Demand

The growing demand for IoT-based energy solutions is driven by the need for energy efficiency, compliance, and data security in user-friendly, adaptable systems.

Key Trends

Integration of IoT and AI in energy management, alongside a growing preference for eco-friendly practices, is reshaping demands for energy efficiency and conservation.

Industry Forces

Economic challenges have led to increased government incentives for energy efficiency, while the manufacturing industry continues to face disruption.

Economic Growth

With economic stabilization and growth, investors are optimistic in the energy sector, particularly towards businesses demonstrating strong, environmentally sustainable models.

Primary Research

To better understand the real-world challenges users face with energy monitoring tools, we conducted interviews with key stakeholders, system integrators, and hardware/software specialists. Our goal was to identify the pain points, unmet needs, and expectations across different roles involved in energy management—from daily operators to decision-makers.

Three Core Problem Areas

💰

Support Gaps and Business Friction

  • Ongoing cost after grants surprises users
  • Poor installation and post-launch service
  • Maintenance is costly without predictive tools
  • Communication breakdowns, creating frustration
"When the grant is over, users are surprised by monthly charges and often abandon the system."
📚

Adoption Barriers, Complexity, Cost & Training

  • Learning new tools takes time and investment
  • Information overload impacts decision-making
  • Skill gaps emerge as technology evolves
"Operators on the shop floor were not highly tech-savvy, requiring some training to interpret information from the dashboards." — Notre Dame AETL
👥

Varying User Expectations & Tech Skills

  • Operators prefer simple, role-specific dashboards
  • Engineers and management need customization and control
  • Expectations of IoT vary significantly by stakeholder
"Customers can have differing expectations and understandings of IoT." — LHP

Ideation & Concept Development

After synthesizing our research and interview insights, we identified a central challenge and entered our ideation phase, blending problem framing and brainstorming in a collaborative, hands-on sprint. We used the Crazy 8's method to quickly explore a wide range of solutions, from AI-powered chat assistants to adaptive dashboards that respond to user roles.

"How might we support manufacturing teams in understanding energy usage and maintaining efficiency after the grant period, without relying on constant external support or complex dashboards?"

Key Patterns That Shaped Our Solution

01

The solution must deliver long-term value, not just display static data.

02

Support and insights should be automated, contextual, and continuous.

03

Different users need role-specific interfaces tailored to their skill level and responsibilities.

04

The system must scale beyond the pilot and reduce reliance on external consultants.

Solution

EcoWise is an advanced energy monitoring platform designed to help manufacturing teams enhance energy efficiency, reduce operational downtime, and accelerate data-driven decision-making. It delivers a unified and intuitive solution for comprehensive operational insight.

With a focus on scalability and ease of use, EcoWise centralizes energy visibility, troubleshooting, and actionable intelligence within a streamlined interface. Its intelligent alert system and integrated AI assistant enable users at all technical levels to manage energy performance confidently, eliminating dependence on external consultants or extensive training.

Features

The EcoWise Dashboard offers a suite of powerful features that empower manufacturers to achieve lasting improvements in energy management and efficiency.

🚀

Streamlined Onboarding & First-Time Setup

New users are welcomed with a guided onboarding flow designed to minimize setup time and build confidence from the start.

  • Walks users through selecting their role and energy priorities
  • Auto-configures dashboard views and alert settings based on preferences
  • Provides a step-by-step tour of key features, including AI Nova and machine-level insights
📊

Dashboard with Machine-Level Focus

The dashboard provides a live snapshot of energy usage across the manufacturing floor, enabling users to monitor, compare, and act on machine performance.

  • Displays energy status and alerts for each machine in a simplified, visual layout
  • Let users click into individual machines for detailed metrics and historical trends
  • Offers filter and sorting options to prioritize what needs attention most
🤖

AI Nova Assistant

AI Nova is a built-in, conversational assistant that provides real-time support, helping users make sense of their energy data and resolve issues more quickly. Nova's mission is to bring clarity at critical moments, illuminating complex insights and guiding users toward confident, informed action.

  • Offers step-by-step troubleshooting for machine alerts and anomalies
  • Learns from plant-level behavior to deliver smarter, personalized guidance
  • Acts as a knowledge hub, answering common questions and reducing reliance on external support
🔔

Smart Alert System: Real-Time Voltage Warnings

Our IoT-powered system continuously monitors electrical health to detect anomalies and prevent issues before they escalate.

  • Detects abnormal behavior like consistently elevated voltage levels
  • Displays critical alerts with clear context—machine name, issue severity, and easy-to-understand explanations
  • Directs users to the integrated Help Center or AI Nova Assistant for instant support and guidance

Business Model

EcoWise is positioned as a scalable B2B SaaS platform designed for industrial clients operating in smart manufacturing environments. Built to support long-term client partnerships, the model is focused on recurring revenue, energy optimization, and operational efficiency.

01

Subscription-Based Tiers

Flexible pricing based on factory size, usage level, and feature needs. Entry-level plans include essential monitoring tools; advanced tiers include AI diagnostics and AI Nova chatbot support.

02

AI-as-a-Service

Premium tools powered by AI Nova to unlock predictive maintenance, root-cause analysis, and proactive alert systems to reduce downtime.

03

Integration Services

Premium onboarding and technical deployment to ensure success. Custom setup, implementation support, and seamless integration with IoT systems and Manufacturing Execution Systems platforms.

04

Data Analytics Add-Ons

Advanced analytics packages tailored for large-scale manufacturers. Benchmarking against industry energy standards, automated compliance reporting, and optimization consulting for cost and performance efficiency.

Reflection

What I Learned

Designing with AI pushed me to think beyond screens. The hardest problem wasn't visual design — it was building user trust in an opaque system. Every design decision had to answer: "Does this make the AI feel more trustworthy or less?" That framing changed how I approached every interaction in the product. I also learned the value of designing for two distinct mental models simultaneously — personalization is not just a nice-to-have, it's sometimes the only path to genuine usefulness.

💭
The solution must scale beyond the consultant — it must empower users to be self-sufficient, confident, and informed in their energy management decisions.
