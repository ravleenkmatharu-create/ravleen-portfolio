Overview

Project Overview

Extinguish is an iOS app designed to simplify the reporting and documentation of environmental hazards and exposures faced by firefighters, supporting long-term health monitoring. Firefighters face a silent crisis — exposure to toxic gases and hazardous materials significantly increases their risk of cancer and respiratory disease. This project set out to address that problem through a lightweight, wearable-integrated mobile solution.

The Problem

Firefighters are exposed to many toxic gases such as Carbon Monoxide (CO) and Hydrogen Cyanide (HCN) and are twice as likely as the general public to develop intestinal, liver, testicular, and prostate cancer. Despite this, existing solutions rely heavily on manual input, which tampers with accuracy and precision.

Root Cause

The problem persists because current tracking relies on manual data entry — which is inaccurate, time-consuming, and often skipped entirely during high-pressure firefighting situations.

Design Challenge

"How might we simplify the process of reporting and documenting environmental hazards and exposures faced by firefighters to support long-term health monitoring?"

Research

Research & Discovery

To gain a deeper understanding of the problem, we conducted 8 interview sessions and 8 observation sessions with firefighters. We also held conversations with the Chief of the fire department and the safety specialist.

01

Secondary Research. Analyzed firefighter health risks, existing PSM and NFORS systems, and identified why current solutions fail due to manual input requirements.

02

Field Interviews. Conducted 8 sessions with firefighters, fire chief, and safety specialist to understand daily pain points, gear constraints, and data tracking workflows.

03

Observation Sessions. Observed 8 sessions of firefighter gear usage, incident reporting workflows, and real-time documentation challenges in the field.

Ideation

Ideation & Brainstorming

We conducted three brainstorming sessions: two sessions of Crazy 8's and one session of ideation with peers to gather diverse perspectives on potential solutions.

01

Crazy 8s

Two rapid ideation sprints exploring 8 solution variations each across onboarding, data capture, reporting, and health features.

02

Peer Ideation

Gathered external perspectives through a collaborative ideation session to validate and refine solution concepts.

03

Feasibility Review

Assessed viability of all concepts against user constraints (gear weight, field usability) and technical limitations.

04

Design Decision

Unanimously selected wearable sensor integration as the core solution — delivering automated data capture without manual entry burden.

Design Direction

Four design themes shaped the solution.

Lightweight Integration Integrate into existing gear — no extra weight on already heavy equipment
Automated Data Entry Eliminate manual logging with sensor-driven, real-time data capture
Simplified Reporting Streamline incident reporting and exposure tracking for field usability
Long-Term Health Enable ongoing monitoring and seamless data sharing with healthcare providers
Solution

Solution

After a thorough feasibility assessment, we designed a firefighter monitoring app that integrates wearable sensors and tracking technology to collect real-time physiological data during firefighting. The system provides insights into health and exposure metrics and enables easy data sharing with healthcare professionals for timely medical guidance.

Four Core Features:

01

Wearable Integration

The app is designed to integrate into the existing suite of firefighter equipment, where wearables and sensors capture physiological data in real-time — avoiding any additional weight burden on already heavy gear.

02 Signature Feature

Autofill & Visual Injury Reporting

Eliminates the need for manual entry of exposure data. The app simplifies documentation with autofill features powered by wearable sensors and enhances comprehension by allowing users to select injured body parts visually.

03

Real-Time Exposure Tracking

The app gathers real-time data regarding exposure to toxic gases through the integrated wearables, providing immediate insight into environmental hazards faced during firefighting operations.

04

Contamination Alerts & Doctor Scheduling

The app not only alerts users about high levels of contamination and toxic gas exposure but also has the ability to schedule appointments with doctors and seamlessly share collected health data for professional medical review.
