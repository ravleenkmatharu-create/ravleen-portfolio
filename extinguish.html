Health Tech Mobile App UX Research Wearables

Extinguish

A firefighter monitoring app integrating wearable sensors to collect real-time exposure data — protecting the people who protect us.

Timeline Fall 2023 · 6 Weeks
Role UX Researcher & UI Designer
Team 4 Design Students
Platform iOS Mobile App
Tools Figma · Miro
Problem Statement
🎯
"How might we simplify the process of reporting and documenting environmental hazards and exposures faced by firefighters to support long-term health monitoring?"
Research

Understanding the Reality

We conducted both secondary and primary research to deeply understand the exposure risks firefighters face and how existing documentation systems were failing them.

Secondary Research

Firefighters are routinely exposed to toxic gases including Carbon Monoxide (CO) and Hydrogen Cyanide (HCN). Studies show they are twice as likely as the general public to develop intestinal, liver, testicular, and prostate cancer. Historical solutions relied heavily on manual input, compromising accuracy and precision.

Primary Research

We conducted 8 interview sessions and 8 observation sessions with active firefighters, held conversations with a fire department Chief and a safety specialist, and synthesized findings through affinity mapping and three brainstorming sessions.

Key Takeaways

What the Research Revealed

Six core design requirements emerged from our research synthesis — each one directly tied to a real frustration or unmet need we heard from firefighters.

01

Eliminate manual exposure data entry to conserve time and minimize effort on scene.

02

Simplify documentation via autofill functionality with a visual body-part selection interface.

03

Prevent additional weight burden on already-heavy protective equipment.

04

Enable real-time toxic gas exposure data collection through existing wearable technology.

05

Provide contamination alerts and direct doctor appointment scheduling capabilities.

06

Support long-term health preservation by making exposure history shareable with medical providers.

Ideation

Brainstorming & Early Concepts

Three brainstorming sessions — two Crazy 8's rounds and a peer ideation session — helped us rapidly explore the solution space before assessing feasibility and committing to a direction.

Design Process

From Insight to Interface

With research findings synthesized, we moved through low-fidelity wireframing and feasibility assessment before developing the high-fidelity designs in Figma.

01

Affinity Mapping

Synthesized interview and observation data to surface patterns across firefighter pain points, behaviors, and unmet needs.

02

Crazy 8's Ideation

Two rapid ideation sessions generated a broad solution space, followed by a peer session to pressure-test ideas.

03

Low-Fidelity Wireframing

Explored core flows with sketches and lo-fi wireframes, assessing solution feasibility before committing to a direction.

04

High-Fidelity Design

Developed polished interactive Figma prototypes following lo-fi iteration — ready for usability testing and handoff.

Solution

App Features & Capabilities

Extinguish integrates with wearables and sensors already used by firefighters — adding intelligence without adding weight or friction to an already demanding job.

📡

Real-Time Data Collection

Gathers physiological and environmental data from wearable sensors, tracking toxic gas exposure levels (CO, HCN) without requiring manual input on scene.

🔔

Health Alerts

Notifies users when contamination levels exceed safe thresholds, ensuring no exposure event goes unlogged or unaddressed.

🏥

Medical Integration

Enables direct appointment scheduling with healthcare providers from within the app, bridging the gap between field exposure and clinical follow-up.

✍️

Documentation Automation

An autofill feature pulls from sensor data to pre-populate exposure reports — reducing manual entry to a review-and-confirm interaction.

🫁

Visual Body-Part Interface

A body-map selection tool lets firefighters indicate affected areas without needing medical vocabulary.

📤

Data Sharing & Clinical Guidance

Exposure history is shareable with healthcare professionals in a structured, medical-friendly format supporting timely clinical decisions.

Final Design

High-Fidelity Screens

The final designs translate all research insights into a cohesive, gesture-aware mobile experience built for the realities of a firefighter's day.

Reflection

What I Learned

This project reinforced how constraints sharpen design. The 6-week timeline forced ruthless prioritization — we had to identify the most valuable feature set and execute it well rather than building everything. Designing for a high-stress user context deepened my appreciation for cognitive load reduction: every screen needed to work for someone who is tired, stressed, and pressed for time. Connecting with real firefighters during research was the most grounding experience — their candor shaped every design decision we made.
