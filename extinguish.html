Health Tech Mobile App UX Research Wearables

Extinguish

A firefighter monitoring app integrating wearable sensors to collect real-time exposure data — protecting the people who protect us.

Timeline Fall 2023 · 6 Weeks
Role UX Researcher & UI Designer
Team 4 Design Students
Platform iOS Mobile App
Tools Figma · Miro
Problem Statement
🎯
"How might we simplify the process of reporting and documenting environmental hazards and exposures faced by firefighters to support long-term health monitoring?"
Research

Understanding the Reality

Secondary Research

Firefighters are routinely exposed to toxic gases including Carbon Monoxide (CO) and Hydrogen Cyanide (HCN). Studies show they are twice as likely as the general public to develop intestinal, liver, testicular, and prostate cancer. Historical solutions relied heavily on manual input, compromising accuracy and precision.

Primary Research

We conducted 8 interview sessions and 8 observation sessions with active firefighters, conversations with a fire department Chief and a safety specialist, and synthesized insights through affinity mapping and three brainstorming sessions.

Key Takeaways

What the Research Revealed

Six core design requirements emerged directly from what we heard and observed in the field.

01

Eliminate manual exposure data entry to conserve time and minimize effort on scene.

02

Simplify documentation via autofill with a visual body-part selection interface.

03

Prevent additional weight burden on already-heavy protective equipment.

04

Enable real-time toxic gas data collection through existing wearable technology.

05

Provide contamination alerts and direct doctor appointment scheduling.

06

Support long-term health preservation by making exposure history shareable with providers.

Design Process

From Insight to Interface

We moved from affinity mapping and Crazy 8's ideation through low-fidelity wireframing before arriving at high-fidelity Figma prototypes.

01

Affinity Mapping

Synthesized interview and observation data to surface patterns across firefighter pain points and unmet needs.

02

Crazy 8's Ideation

Two rapid ideation sessions generated a broad solution space, followed by a peer session to pressure-test ideas.

03

Low-Fidelity Wireframing

Explored core flows with lo-fi wireframes, assessing feasibility before committing to a direction.

04

High-Fidelity Design

Developed polished interactive Figma prototypes ready for usability testing and developer handoff.

Solution

App Features & Capabilities

Extinguish integrates with wearables already used by firefighters — adding intelligence without adding weight or friction to an already demanding job.

Onboarding & device connection — pairing wearable sensors to the app

Health reporting — exposure history graphs and long-term trend tracking

Incident logging — quick-log flow with success confirmation

Medical integration — doctor appointment scheduling flow

Exposure tracking — detailed incident log and health timeline

Reflection

What I Learned

This project reinforced how constraints sharpen design. The 6-week timeline forced ruthless prioritization — we had to identify the most valuable feature set and execute it well. Designing for a high-stress user context deepened my appreciation for cognitive load reduction: every screen needed to work for someone who is tired, stressed, and pressed for time. Connecting with real firefighters during research was the most grounding experience — their candor shaped every design decision we made.
