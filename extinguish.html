Extinguish

Extinguish is an app designed to simplify the process of reporting and documenting environmental hazards and exposures faced by Firefighters to support long-term health monitoring.

Role UX Researcher & UI Designer
Timeline 6 Weeks — Fall 2023
Team Collaborated with 3 other design students
Platform iOS Mobile App
Tools Figma · Miro

Secondary Research

Firefighters are at risk of various health issues, including cancers and respiratory problems due to exposure to smoke and hazardous materials. Through our secondary research we want to understand the severity of the problem, why it persists and also what past efforts have been done to address it.

We found out that firefighters are many toxic gases such as Carbon Monoxide (CO) and Hydrogen Cyanide (HCN) and are twice as likely as the general public to develop intestinal, liver, testicular, and prostate cancer.

After this, we tried to understand why this problem persists by examining the past efforts that were made to address this issue. Major drawback is that it relies heavily on manual input which tampers with accuracy and precision.

Primary Research

To gain a deeper understanding of the problem, we conducted 8 interview sessions and 8 observation sessions with firefighters. Additionally, we held conversations with the Chief of the fire department and the safety specialist.

After collecting the data, we decided to analyze it through Affinity Mapping, to gather the insights from our research. From this, we derived our key takeaways:

01

Avoiding any additional weight burden on their already heavy gear.

02

Eliminating the need for manual entry of exposure data to save time and reduce effort.

03

Simplifying the process of reporting and tracking exposure incidents.

04

Ensuring the ability to safeguard their long-term health.

Through this research analysis, we formulated our "How might we..." problem statement to guide our focus towards finding the ideal solution for this problem.

"How might we simplify the process of reporting and documenting environmental hazards and exposures faced by firefighters to support long-term health monitoring?"

Brainstorming

We conducted three brainstorming sessions, consisting of two sessions of Crazy 8's and one session of ideation with peers to gather their perspectives on the solution for the problem.

After conducting a thorough analysis and assessing the feasibility of our solutions, we made a unanimous decision to settle on one design idea. Our solutions' feasibility unanimously made the decision to, designing a firefighter monitoring app that integrates wearable sensors and tracking technology to collect real-time physiological data during firefighting.

The system's objective is to offer insights into health and exposure metrics, and the ease of sharing the data with healthcare professionals. This enables timely medical guidance and testing recommendations, enhancing firefighters' safety and well-being.

Features

Let's see, how our MVP addresses the key takeaways we identified during the data analysis stage.

01

Avoiding any additional weight burden on their already heavy gear.

The app is designed to be integrated into the existing suite of firefighters, where wearables and sensors are used to avoid any extra weight.

02

Eliminating the need for manual entry of exposure data to save time and reduce effort.

The app simplifies the documentation process with its autofill feature and enhances comprehension by allowing users to select the injured body part visually.

03

Simplifying the process of reporting and tracking exposure incidents.

The app gathers real-time data regarding exposure to toxic gases through the wearables.

04

Ensuring the ability to safeguard their long-term health.

The app not only alerts users about high levels of contamination but also has the ability to schedule appointments with doctors.
